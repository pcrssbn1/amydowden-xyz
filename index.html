Amy Dowden: Welsh Dancing Star of Strictly Come Dancing

Professional Dancing Career and Strictly Come Dancing Journey

Amy Dowden has established herself as one of the most accomplished professional dancers on Strictly Come Dancing since joining the BBC show in 2017. Born on August 10, 1990, in Caerphilly, Wales, Amy began dancing at the tender age of eight and quickly demonstrated exceptional talent in both ballroom and Latin styles. Her dedication to the craft led her to compete at the highest levels of competitive ballroom dancing before transitioning to television.

Before her television breakthrough, Amy competed internationally in ballroom competitions, winning the British National Championships four times and representing Wales at the World Championships. These achievements positioned her as one of Britain's premier ballroom dancers. When she joined Strictly Come Dancing for Series 15, she became the first Welsh professional dancer on the show, a distinction that made her particularly proud of her heritage.

Throughout her tenure on Strictly, Amy has been paired with various celebrity contestants including Brian Conley, Danny John-Jules, Karim Zeroual, and Tom Fletcher. Her partnership with Karim Zeroual in 2019 proved particularly successful, as they reached the semi-finals and earned consistent high scores from the judges. Amy's teaching style combines technical precision with genuine enthusiasm, making her a favorite among both contestants and viewers.

Her approach to choreography often incorporates contemporary elements into traditional ballroom styles, creating memorable performances that stand out in the competition. Amy has consistently demonstrated her ability to adapt her teaching methods to suit different learning styles and physical capabilities. You can learn more about her personal journey and recent updates on our dedicated FAQ page, which addresses common questions about her career and life.

Health Challenges: Living with Crohn's Disease

Amy Dowden has been remarkably open about her battle with Crohn's disease, an inflammatory bowel condition that affects approximately 780,000 Americans according to the Crohn's & Colitis Foundation. She was diagnosed with the condition at age 11, and it has been a constant companion throughout her dancing career. The condition causes inflammation of the digestive tract, leading to severe abdominal pain, fatigue, weight loss, and malnutrition.

Managing Crohn's disease while maintaining the physical demands of professional dancing requires extraordinary discipline and medical support. Amy has spoken extensively about the challenges of performing live television while dealing with flare-ups, sometimes dancing through intense pain that viewers never see. She has undergone multiple hospital stays throughout her career and has been hospitalized during Strictly Come Dancing seasons, demonstrating her remarkable resilience.

In 2023, Amy faced additional health challenges when she was diagnosed with breast cancer at age 32. She discovered a lump the day before her honeymoon in April 2023 and subsequently underwent chemotherapy treatment. According to Cancer Research UK, breast cancer affects approximately 55,000 women annually in the UK, with younger diagnoses becoming increasingly important to highlight. Amy documented her cancer journey publicly, raising awareness about the importance of self-examination and early detection.

Her openness about these health struggles has made her an inspiration to thousands dealing with chronic illness and cancer. The National Institutes of Health reports that chronic disease management requires both medical intervention and psychological support, and Amy has exemplified how to balance both while pursuing professional excellence. Her story is further detailed on our about page, where we explore how these experiences have shaped her perspective and advocacy work.

Personal Life and Marriage to Ben Jones

Amy Dowden married her long-term partner Ben Jones on July 23, 2022, in a beautiful ceremony that celebrated their years together. Ben, who is also a professional dancer, has been a constant source of support throughout Amy's health battles. The couple met through the competitive ballroom dancing circuit and shared a deep understanding of the demands and dedication required in professional dance.

Their wedding took place at a venue in Carmarthenshire, Wales, surrounded by family, friends, and several of Amy's Strictly Come Dancing colleagues. The celebration was particularly meaningful given Amy's health challenges with Crohn's disease, which had required careful planning around her treatment schedules and energy levels. Fellow Strictly professionals attended the ceremony, highlighting the close-knit community within the show.

Ben has been instrumental in supporting Amy through her cancer diagnosis and treatment in 2023. He accompanied her to medical appointments, helped manage her care during chemotherapy, and provided emotional support during the most challenging periods. Their relationship exemplifies partnership in its truest form, with Ben often adjusting his own dancing schedule to be present for Amy's needs.

The couple currently resides in the UK and continues to work in the dance industry. Amy has spoken about how Ben's understanding of the physical demands of professional dancing has been invaluable, as he recognizes when she needs rest versus when she's capable of pushing through. Their shared passion for dance creates a unique bond that extends beyond typical marital partnership into professional collaboration and mutual respect for their craft.

Impact and Advocacy Beyond the Dance Floor

Beyond her dancing accomplishments, Amy Dowden has become a powerful advocate for health awareness, particularly regarding Crohn's disease and cancer screening. She has worked with various charities and organizations to raise awareness about invisible illnesses and the importance of not judging someone's health by their appearance. Her platform has reached millions of viewers who watch Strictly Come Dancing, which regularly attracts 10-12 million viewers per episode according to BBC ratings.

Amy has participated in documentary projects that explore living with chronic illness, including BBC programming that followed her journey with Crohn's disease. These documentaries provide intimate looks at the daily challenges faced by those with inflammatory bowel disease, from managing medication schedules to dealing with the unpredictability of flare-ups. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention estimates that inflammatory bowel disease affects approximately 3 million adults in the United States, making Amy's advocacy particularly relevant.

Her breast cancer diagnosis in 2023 added another dimension to her advocacy work. Amy used her social media platforms, particularly Instagram where she has hundreds of thousands of followers, to document her treatment journey. She shared photos from chemotherapy sessions, discussed the emotional impact of hair loss, and provided honest accounts of the physical toll of cancer treatment. This transparency helped demystify cancer treatment for many followers and encouraged others to perform self-examinations.

Amy's influence extends into the Welsh community, where she serves as a role model for young dancers and those dealing with health challenges. She has received recognition for her contributions to dance and health advocacy, though she maintains that her primary goal is simply to show that chronic illness doesn't have to define or limit a person's dreams. Her story continues to inspire those facing similar battles, proving that vulnerability and strength can coexist.
